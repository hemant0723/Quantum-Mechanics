Classical Lorentz force from Hamiltonian; probability current with vector potential

Problem 1

Show that the Hamiltonian

$$ H = \frac{1}{2m}\Big[\vec p + \frac{e}{c}\,\vec A(\vec r,t)\Big]^2 - \frac{e^2}{r} $$

yields the Lorentz force equation

$$ m\,\frac{d^{2}\vec r}{dt^{2}} = -e\Big[\vec E(\vec r,t) + \frac{1}{c}\,\frac{d\vec r}{dt}\times\vec B(\vec r,t)\Big]. $$

Hint.

$$ \frac{d}{dt}\vec A(\vec r,t) = \frac{\partial \vec A}{\partial t} + \frac{dx}{dt}\frac{\partial \vec A}{\partial x} + \frac{dy}{dt}\frac{\partial \vec A}{\partial y} + \frac{dz}{dt}\frac{\partial \vec A}{\partial z}, $$ $$ \frac{dx}{dt} = \frac{\partial H}{\partial p_x}, \qquad \frac{dp_x}{dt} = -\,\frac{\partial H}{\partial x}. $$

Problem 2

Show that for a system described by the Hamiltonian

$$ H = \frac{1}{2m}\Big[\vec p + \frac{e}{c}\,\vec A(\vec r,t)\Big]^2, $$

the probability current density $\vec j$, which satisfies

$$ \frac{\partial}{\partial t}\big(\psi^*\psi\big) + \nabla\cdot\vec j = 0, $$

is given by

$$ \vec j = \frac{\hbar}{2 i m}\left[\psi^* \, \nabla\psi - (\nabla\psi^*)\,\psi + \frac{2 i e}{\hbar c}\,\vec A(\vec r,t)\,\psi^*\psi\right]. $$
