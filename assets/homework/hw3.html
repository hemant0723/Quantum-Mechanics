(1) Properties of Hermitian Operators

(a) Orthogonality for distinct eigenvalues

Let \(A=A^\dagger\) (component form \(A^\dagger_{ij}=A^{*}_{ji}\)). Suppose \[ A\,\psi_1(x)=a_1\,\psi_1(x), \qquad \big[A\,\psi_2(x)\big]^*=a_2\,\psi_2^*(x). \] Prove that \[ \int \psi_2^*(x)\,\psi_1(x)\,dx = 0 \quad \text{if } a_1\neq a_2 . \]

(b) Hermitian conjugate and product rule

Define \(A^\dagger\) by \[ \int dx\,\big(A\psi(x)\big)^*\,\phi(x)=\int dx\,\psi^*(x)\,\big(A^\dagger\phi(x)\big). \] For \(A=A^\dagger\) show \((AB)^\dagger=B^\dagger A^\dagger\). In general, \((AB\cdots Z)^\dagger = Z^\dagger\cdots B^\dagger A^\dagger\).

(2) Algebra with \([A,A^\dagger]=\hbar\)

Let \(f(A^\dagger)\) be any polynomial (or power series) in \(A^\dagger\) and assume \([A,A^\dagger]=\hbar\) (a c-number). Define \[ U_n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{n!}} \left(\frac{A^\dagger}{\sqrt{\hbar}}\right)^{\!n} U_0, \qquad A\,U_0=0 . \] Prove:

  1. Derivative form: \[ A\,f(A^\dagger)\,U_0 = \hbar\,\frac{d f(A^\dagger)}{dA^\dagger}\,U_0, \] i.e. \(A\equiv \hbar\,\dfrac{d}{dA^\dagger}\) when acting on \(f(A^\dagger)U_0\).

  2. Similarity transform: \[ e^{\lambda A}\,f(A^\dagger)\,e^{-\lambda A} = f\!\big(A^\dagger+\lambda\hbar\big). \] Hint: \[ e^{\lambda A} A^\dagger e^{-\lambda A} = A^\dagger + \lambda[A,A^\dagger] + \frac{\lambda^2}{2!}[A,[A,A^\dagger]]+\cdots = A^\dagger+\lambda\hbar . \]

Corollary (BCH for this algebra): If \([A,B]\) is a c-number commuting with \(A,B\), \[ e^{A+B}=e^{A}\,e^{B}\,e^{-\tfrac{1}{2}[A,B]}. \] In particular, with \(B=\beta A^\dagger\) and \([A,A^\dagger]=\hbar\), \[ e^{\alpha A+\beta A^\dagger} = e^{\alpha A}\,e^{\beta A^\dagger}\,e^{-\tfrac{1}{2}\alpha\beta\,\hbar}. \]
