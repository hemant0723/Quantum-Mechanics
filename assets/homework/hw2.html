Homework 2

Parity and 1D scattering with the $2\times 2$ S‑matrix

1) Parity operator

Show that the parity operator satisfies $ \hat{P}\,\psi(x) = \psi(-x) $ and is real for any state $\psi(x)$.

2) Localized potential and the scattering matrix

Consider an arbitrary potential localized on a finite part of the $x$‑axis. The solutions to the left and to the right of the potential are given by

$ Ae^{ikx} + Be^{-ikx} \;\text{(left)} \qquad Ce^{ikx} + De^{-ikx} \;\text{(right)} $

Respectively, show that if we write

$ C = S_{11}A + S_{12}D, \qquad B = S_{21}A + S_{22}D. $

That is, relate the outgoing waves to the incoming wave by

\[ \begin{pmatrix} C \\ B \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} A \\ D \end{pmatrix}. \]

Show that the following relations hold:

\[ |S_{11}|^2 + |S_{12}|^2 = 1, \qquad |S_{21}|^2 + |S_{22}|^2 = 1, \qquad S_{11}^* S_{12} + S_{21}^* S_{22} = 0. \]

i.e., $ S = \begin{pmatrix} S_{11} & S_{12} \\ S_{21} & S_{22} \end{pmatrix} $ is unitary, so $S^{\dagger}S = I$.
